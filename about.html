About

click to download my CV (.pdf)

I am currently a postdoctoral research and education fellow at Amherst College, which is part of the Five College Astronomy Department. My position involves both research and teaching upper-division observational astronomy courses (for more on this unique postdoctoral position, click here!), which gives me great opportunities to work with and mentor excellent undergraduate student researchers.

My research involves low-mass stars, brown dwarfs, and exoplanets, studied with observational techniques including adaptive optics imaging, UV to NIR spectroscopy, and submillimeter interfermetry. To read more about some of the current research projects I am working on, check out the Research page.

At Amherst, I also work closely with Prof. Kate Follette and her research group on direct imaging and characterization of transition disk systems and accreting protoplanets. See more about the Follette Lab here: Follette Research Group.
